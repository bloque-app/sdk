# Principles — Personality, Voice-in-Design, Rules

The soul of the system. Tokens alone don't make a page feel like Bloque — these principles do.

## Brand personality

> **Premium, engineered, trustworthy.** Bloque is a precision instrument for moving money. Not playful, not corporate, not crypto-bro. The feeling of a well-machined tool — confidence radiating from competence.

Design goal: **excitement within 5 seconds** — *"holy shit, I need this."* Not hype. Earned excitement from seeing something genuinely well-built that solves a real problem.

Audience: **mixed** — developers (indie hackers, startup engineers, AI builders) AND non-technical decision-makers (CFOs, finance leads, ops). Every surface must speak to both without dumbing down for either.

## Five design principles

1. **Density signals competence.** Don't hide information behind "simplicity." Show the product's depth confidently. Empty hero sections with one sentence and a button read as marketing — Bloque reads as a tool.

2. **The form IS the product.** When we show qualification flows, pricing configurators, or SDK demos, the interaction itself should feel like using Bloque — not like filling out a form to learn about Bloque.

3. **Cool dark, purple-tinted.** Every surface carries the `#0d0c17` purple undertone. Deep, premium, consistent. Never pure black, never neutral gray, never navy.

4. **Surgical accent.** Violet `#a78bfa` appears in exactly **2–3 places per viewport**. If you see a fourth, remove one. The accent only works when it's rare.

5. **Mixed audience, single register.** Write for smart people. Technical enough to earn developer respect, clear enough that a CFO gets it. Copy and design must both honor this rule.

## The three anti-references

Bloque is defined as much by what it isn't as what it is. Never let a surface drift toward any of these:

### ❌ NOT Web3 / crypto
- No neon pink/green gradients
- No "to the moon" energy
- No glassmorphism for its own sake
- No 3D chrome / glossy effects
- No degen typography (Orbitron, Space Grotesk set too loud)

### ❌ NOT generic SaaS template
- No hero → 3-column features → pricing grid cliché
- No stock illustrations of smiling cartoon people
- No "empower your business" copy
- No pastel gradient CTA buttons
- No unearned testimonial carousels

### ❌ NOT traditional banking
- No navy blue anywhere
- No stock photos of handshakes or skyscrapers
- No "trust" iconography (shields, locks as decoration)
- No stuffy serif headlines set large
- No beige / cream / conservative palette

## The voice-in-design principle

The copy voice IS part of the design. A Bloque page without a compressed-tracking headline that uses one italic accent word will feel wrong even if every token is correct. Examples:

- *Finance is the **last** frontier.*
- *The builder of your financial stack should be **you**.*
- *Design **your own**.*

The italic word takes the accent color. It's the smallest, most consistent expression of the surgical-accent rule.

## What "precision instrument" looks like, concretely

- Tight alignment. Elements line up on clear grids — not rough approximations.
- Consistent tracking. The same headline size always uses the same negative letter-spacing.
- Calm motion. Transitions are 150–250ms, ease-out, no bounce, no overshoot.
- No stock iconography. If an icon appears, it's line-based, 1.5–1.75px stroke, muted foreground color, never the accent.
- No filler. If a section doesn't do work for the page, cut it.

## When you're stuck

Ask: *would Linear ship this?* Linear is the reference, not because we're copying them but because they share the "precision instrument" feeling. If the answer is no, the design is drifting.

Then ask: *does this make a smart skeptical CFO think "oh, real engineers built this"?* If the answer is no, you've drifted toward SaaS template or crypto.
